        Caution

      You must have six cards in your hand after your turn.

      Market Open cards (10) enables the player to add peddles of cannabis to their stash (provided there is no heat). In order to end the current hand/round, any player may play a Market Close (5) on their own Market Open (provided there is no heat). The money for the round is totalled and a new hand is dealt to each player.

      Peddle Cards (28)

      Peddle cards are cards which make up the money in the game, indicating the sale of cannabis to a particular value. There are six types: Home grown (6), Mexico (6), Colombia (5), Jamaica (5), Panama (5), and Dr. Feelgood (1).

      Protection Cards (6)

      Protection cards allow a player to protect their peddle cards against skim cards. There are three types: Catch a buzz (2), Grab a snack (2), and Lust conquers all (2). Dr. Feelgood, the highest valued peddle card, may never be protected.

      Any combination of unprotected peddle cards can be protected so long as they do not exceed the protection value.

      Heat On Cards (12)

      Heat on cards represent police activity and are made up of 4 different varieties: Bust (3), Detained (3), Felony (3), and Search & seizure (3). A Heat on card played on another player's "Market open" card prevents that player from peddling cannabis (until the corresponding "Heat off" card is played).

      Heat Off Cards (24)

      Heat off cards are made up of Bust (5), Detained (5), Felony (5), and Search & seizure (5). Any of these cards played on the corresponding "Heat on" card will remove the heat and enable the player to continue putting down Peddle cards.

      Pay fine/Heat Off cards (4) are played somewhat differently from the four other Heat Off cards.
      When played on your "heated" Hassle Pile it may be used to "un-heat" yourself by paying a fine of the smallest tabled Peddle card in your Stash Pile to the Wasted Pile! (If you have no Peddle cards in your Stash Pile you may not take the heat off with this card.)

      Nirvana Cards (6)

      Nirvana cards give bonuses to players. There are two types: Stonehigh (5) and Euphoria (1). Either of these cards will provide extra turns (Stonehigh: 1 turn. Euphoria: 2 turns) and cancel any "heat on" card active on the player. In addition, the player receives a peddle card from every other player: Stonehigh requires every player to hand over their lowest tabled peddle, whereas Euphoria requires player to hand over their highest tabled peddle.

      Paranoia Cards (8)

      Paranoia cards give penalties to players and come in three sickening varieties: Sold out (4), Doublecrossed (3) and Utterly wiped out (1).

      • If any of these cards are played on the Wasted Pile, Sold out forces the player to skip a turn and lose their lowest unprotected peddle; Doublecrossed forces the player to skip two turns and lose their highest unprotected peddle; and Utterly wiped out forces the player to skip two turns, put all peddle cards (including unprotected) on the Wasted Pile and must reopen with a new Market Open card.

      • Whenever any of these cards are played on the Wasted Pile, each player must pass a card from their hand to their neighbor: this can allow the play of one of the less dangerous Paranoia cards to enable a player to pass on a more dangerous one.

      • If the cards are found unplayed at the end of a hand, a score penalty is assessed: $25,000 for Sold out, $50,000 for Doublecrossed, and $100,000 for Utterly wiped out.

      Skim Cards (5)

      Skim cards allow a player to steal peddle cards on the table from other players. Steal your neighbor's pot (4) allows the player to take any peddle card from another player, and The Banker (1) allows the player to steal 20 percent of every player's unprotected tabled peddle money at the end of the round.

      You cannot steal money from an opponent if you or they have heat on.
